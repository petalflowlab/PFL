Evolution

Getting ready for a V2. that moves with you.

Built From Scratch. Ready to Be Built Right.

Every single part of the Petal Staff was designed from the ground up — the petal heads, the electronics, the assembly. Nothing off the shelf. The prototype has been torn apart and rebuilt more times than we can count, which makes for an incredible demo prop. But a demo prop and a prop built for your hands are two very different things.

After all the V1 lessons, there are three things that need to be right before I'd call a staff your reliable flow buddy.

🌸 Silicone Molded Petals

The smaller petals have held up beautifully — smashes, drops, contact staff abuse, poi life — they've taken it all without complaint. The larger hybrid dragon petals are a different story. For some spinners they've met their breaking point, which honestly was always the risk with that size.

The good news? They're easily swapped out. But for V2 we're going further than a replacement. Silicone molded petals are coming in to replace the 3D printed TPU, and the difference in toughness is significant. More robust, more reliable, and built to keep up with however hard you play.

📡 Motion-Sensitive LEDs

A custom PCB with a 3-axis accelerometer and gyroscope tracks your exact speed, tilt, and rotation in real time. The LEDs respond to what you're actually doing — not a preset loop. Fast spin triggers a bloom. A slow roll closes it back down. It knows the difference.

Engineering Backbone

The first batch of capsules survived a year of hard drops without issue — proving the design holds up. The one thing that needed fixing was the LED connection point, which was the weak link. The redesign sorts that out, giving the LEDs a secure connection and making the whole capsule more robust than ever.

Every staffer knows the struggle — breaking down a staff for travel is never fun. V2 is getting a 3-piece design to make that a whole lot easier to live with. The split also solved something else entirely. With onboard motion sensors, having the chip sitting on one side only was creating headaches with precise detection. The 3-piece layout allows independent battery and sensor placement on each section — perfectly balanced, and with better battery life to match. It adds a little to the cost, but the payoff in accuracy and portability makes it a no-brainer.

Industry standard tough. The 3-piece design with long overlaps avoids flexing problems, ensuring the electronics inside stay protected no matter what.

More LEDs meant more power drain, so a full-size 18650 battery was chosen to add weight and provide enough power. Our target is at least 10 hours of flow on a charge — USB-C charging won't be needed til morning.

A built-in microphone syncs the LEDs to the music around you in real time. Beat drops trigger flashes, pulses, and reactive waves across the full LED strip. Dial the sensitivity through the app and let the room control your lights.

What Is This Kickstarter Campaign Structure?

These are completely custom-built props — from the petal head design to the electronics to final assembly. Every part of it came from scratch. During prototyping, they've been taken apart and put back together again and again, which makes a kickass demo prop but unsuitable for your hands.

From prototype to final durable reliable staff needs two dramatic changes. Redesigned PCB layout to strengthen LED attachment point and stronger silicone molded Big Flower Staff Petals. . All components from previous capsule is remaining the same, only the aforenentioned structural changes are being made so the year of testing still holds up.

  • 1. Direct PCB Connection — Strengthened connection of LED strip to board for zero wiggle (fixing V1 wiggles).
  • 2. Silicone Molded Petals — Superior strength to guaranteed they survive the hard life of drops (fixing prototype casualties).
  • 3. 3-Piece Design — Remaining stiff using polycarbonate overlap while adding portability and independent battery/sensor tracking.
